# Design Rule: Golden Ratio

## Principle
The golden ratio (phi = 1.618...) is a mathematical ratio found in nature that creates visually harmonious compositions. Logos based on the golden ratio convey balance and natural beauty.

## Application to Logo

### Golden Ratio (1:1.618)
- The relationship between width and height follows phi
- Internal elements sized with multiples of phi
- Spacing between elements based on phi
- Text sizes proportional via phi

### Fibonacci Spiral
- The golden spiral guides the logo's visual flow
- Elements positioned along the spiral
- Focal point at the center of the spiral
- Organic growth of elements following the sequence

### Golden Rectangle
- Logo container with phi proportions
- Internal subdivisions following recursive golden rectangles
- Each subdivision can contain a design element
- Squares and rectangles derived from the main rectangle

### Golden Circles
- Circles with diameters in Fibonacci proportion (1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13...)
- Golden circle intersections define anchor points
- Fibonacci circle arcs create natural curves

### Instruction
The logo must be constructed using the golden ratio (1:1.618) as the basis for all proportions. Elements, spacing, and shapes must follow the phi ratio. The result must convey natural harmony, balance, and mathematical beauty.
