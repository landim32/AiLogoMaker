# Design Rule: Flat Design

## Principle
Flat design eliminates three-dimensional elements such as shadows, gradients, and textures, opting for solid colors, 2D shapes, and absolute visual simplicity. Modern, clean, and digital-first.

## Application to Logo

### Colors
- Solid and flat colors, no gradients
- Vibrant but controlled palette
- Strong contrast between elements
- No projected or internal shadows
- No glow or reflection effects

### Shapes
- Pure 2D geometry
- Sharp and defined edges
- No perspective or depth
- Overlapping shapes with transparency or clipping
- Consistent rounded corners (when used)

### Typography
- Clean sans-serif fonts
- Consistent weight
- No text effects (shadow, outline, gradient)
- Hierarchy through size and color, not style

### Iconography
- Icons simplified to the maximum
- Uniform stroke or solid fill
- Clear and direct visual metaphors
- Consistent style across all icons

### Composition
- Grid alignment
- Generous spacing
- Clear visual hierarchy
- Balance between elements

### Instruction
The logo must follow flat design principles. Use exclusively solid colors, 2D shapes, and clean geometry. No gradients, shadows, textures, or 3D effects. The result must be modern, clean, and perfectly adapted for digital interfaces.
